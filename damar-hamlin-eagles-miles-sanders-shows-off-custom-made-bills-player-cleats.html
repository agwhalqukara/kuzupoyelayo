Damar Hamlin: Eagles' Miles Sanders shows off custom-made Bills player cleats

August 2024 · 2 minute read

Philadelphia Eagles running back Miles Sanders has paid tribute to Damar Hamlin by showing off custom-made cleats ahead of the team’s NFL Conference title game against the San Francisco 49ers on Sunday. 

The 25-year-old proudly shared a clip of his new boots on his Instagram profile on Thursday ahead of Eagles practice. The tip of his left shoe displays Hamlin’s jersey No. 3 as well as the Bills safety’s renown ‘Did we win?’ question he asked doctors after waking up from a cardiac arrest he suffered during the Bills-Bengals game on January 2. 

Sanders’ right boot features an illustration of Hamlin, painted within the shape of a heart. The cleats are both covered in white, red and blue, which is the colors of the Buffalo Bills.  

Eagles RB Miles Sanders shared some custom-made, Damar Hamlin-themed cleats at practice

Hamlin re-posted the Philly player’s Instagram story on his, describing the boots as ‘unreal’ 

It’s unclear if Sanders will be allowed to wear the footwear against the San Francisco 49ers

The lines ‘Love For Damar’ and ‘Chasing M’s’ – Hamlin’s foundation, which raised more than $9million while the 24-year-old was in hotomspital – are also scribed on the footwear. 

Hamlin was clearly touched by the work of art and shared Sanders’ video on his own Instagram, along with the caption: ‘Unreal Troob’ with a heart and mind blown emoji.

It’s unclear if Sanders will get to wear the custom-made, Hamlin-themed cleats on Sunday against the Niners. 

Hamlin’s been given a wide array of support from NFL players since his January 2 collapse

The Bills safety has also told fans that they’ll hear from his soon, thanking their support online

But, other NFL players have also shown acts of kindness towards the 2021 NFL Draft pick, including QB legend Tom Brady, who donated $10,000 to Hamlin’s charity – a toy drive foundation.

A mural honoring the Bills safety also sparked attention of the player himself as he continues to recover from his on-field medical emergency two weeks ago.  

Meanwhile, Hamlin’s doctors in Cincinnati were given a key to the city by the Mayor ‘for their lifesaving care’ of the NFL player. 

Hamlin has also promised fans they will ‘hear from him soon’ as the Bills safety prepares to speak publicly for the first time since his health-related incident, which sparked conspiracy theories after his showing at the Bills-Bengals game on January 22nd.
